Story of my life

Chapter 1
Who I Am

I am an Italian-born Chinese designer who was born and raised in Prato, a small Tuscan city with a big textile heart. Growing up between cultures, I was constantly navigating different languages, values, and expectations. That in-between space shaped how I see the world. It made me curious, observant, empathetic — and, sometimes, a bit restless. I always felt there was more to understand.

As a kid, I loved creating — drawing, writing, imagining stories. But I was equally drawn to structure, to logic, to the quiet intensity of philosophy. Looking back, it makes sense that I found my way to design — a space where imagination meets method, and ideas become something you can touch.

If I had to describe myself in a few words: creative, curious, a little bit of a passionate nerd — and yes, a perfectionist. I care deeply about what I do. Sometimes maybe too much. But I’ve come to see that as a strength. Caring is what keeps me engaged. It’s what pushes me to do better.

Chapter 2
How I Fell in Love with Design

My first encounter with design was hands-on — at Liceo Artistico Umberto Brunelleschi, where we learned the fundamentals. Layouts, colors, typefaces, software. It was like learning a new alphabet — one made of form and balance. Back then, design felt like a toolbox: precise, visual, technical.

That toolbox expanded when I entered the Accademia di Belle Arti di Bologna. Suddenly, design wasn’t just about making things look good — it was about making things matter. I started to see it as a language. A way of expressing ideas, telling stories, and even questioning norms. I discovered how aesthetics could carry meaning. How beauty could also be bold.

Still, something was missing. I knew how to craft visuals. But I wanted to go deeper —

to design things that do something, not just look good. I wanted design to have purpose.

Chapter 3
When Design Started Asking Bigger Questions

Toward the end of my BA, I discovered design thinking — and everything shifted. Design became less about making things, and more about making sense.

It was about empathy, curiosity, and asking what if?

That led me to Politecnico di Milano, where I began to see design as a way of acting responsibly in a complex, digital world. I learned to collaborate, lead, and listen — not just as a designer, but as a researcher.

My thesis explores how AI can offer emotionally meaningful companionship without replacing human connection. It’s about trust, empathy, and designing systems that care.

This also connects to my work as a Research Apprentice at the Design Intelligences Lab, where I explore relationships between humans, AI, and more-than-human systems

Chapter 4
Life, Like Bagels

One of my hobbies is making bagels. It might sound random, but I enjoy the process — mixing, shaping, waiting, and baking. It’s not always perfect, but it’s satisfying to see small improvements each time.

For me, it’s similar to design: it takes time, experimentation, and patience. Sometimes things don’t work as planned, but you learn from it. And when it comes out right, it’s a quiet reminder that even small steps and retries matter.
